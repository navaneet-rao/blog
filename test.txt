You don’t want to depend on a crate that depends on packages that only exist on the author’s local filesystem.</p><p id=\"2591\" class=\"pw-post-body-paragraph xh xi sq xj b tn xk xl xm tp xn xo xp xq xr xs xt xu xv xw xx xy xz ya yb yc li bx\" data-selectable-paragraph=\"\" style=\"box-sizing: inherit; margin: 2.14em 0px -0.46em; font-weight: 400; color: rgb(36, 36, 36); word-break: break-word; font-style: normal; line-height: 32px; letter-spacing: -0.003em; font-family: source-serif-pro, Georgia, Cambria, &quot;Times New Roman&quot;, Times, serif; font-size: 20px; font-variant-ligatures: normal; font-variant-caps: normal; orphans: 2; text-align: start; text-indent: 0px; text-transform: none; widows: 2; word-spacing: 0px; -webkit-text-stroke-width: 0px; white-space: normal; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255); text-decoration-thickness: initial; text-decoration-style: initial; text-decoration-color: initial;\">However, many developers break large projects down into smaller modules naturally, and you can’t publish a parent crate that has sub-crates that<span>&nbsp;</span><em class=\"abo\" style=\"box-sizing: inherit; font-style: italic;\">only exist within itself.<span>&nbsp;</span></em>You can’t even publish a crate that has local dev dependencies.
